As a basketball player, your ball-handling can always use work. The most effective way to work on both hands at once, especially your weaker side, is doing drills that involve handling two balls like Steph Curry and other great guards of our time practice. Two ball dribbling is what you need to do to get your game to the next level and improve exponentially, even after the 1st day. When starting off with two balls, it will take some getting used to, but if you develop great control over two basketballs, imagine what you will be able to do on the basketball court with one.

For demonstrations of drills watch videos.

Double Ball Dribbling Drills (basic)

To warm up, begin by pounding both balls at the same time at a little above waist height. Make sure that you are in an athletic position as shown in the two pictures above. Continue pounding the balls above waist height for about 20 seconds, then switch to dribbling at your knees. After another 20 seconds, switch to dribbling the balls at your ankles for 20 seconds. Repeat this again in any order you want.

Next, begin dribbling both balls from side-to-side in front of you in sync. Then do this alternating. After, dribble both balls on your side from front-to-back. Alternate between these reps of 20 seconds.

Basic Zig-Zag Drills- Hold one ball like a football in one hand and have one ball ready to dribble. For the first round, you should be doing crossovers. Second round, do a through the legs. Third, do a behind the back. Take one dribble to your right, and do the move while switching the held ball to the other hand. Then explode to your left and do the move again and face left. Do this drill for a court length and then head back.

Around One Leg- Dribble one ball stationary and the other ball around one leg. Alternate sides.

Crossover Juggle- Crossover with one ball and toss the other ball to the other hand. Do this quickly for about 15 seconds. Then change sides.

Two Ball Attack Behind the Back Drill- The player will start on the baseline with a basketball in each hand. They will start to dribble both basketballs at the same time. When the drill starts they will move forward up the floor and make a behind the back with one ball and a crossover with the other ball at the same time at the free throw line, half court, and opposite free throw line (alternate which hand makes the move each time). After each move they need to push the basketballs out in front of them, simulating getting past the defender. When they reach the opposite baseline they will turn around and come back.

Two Ball Attack Through the Legs Drill- The player will start on the baseline with a basketball in each hand. They will start to dribble both basketballs at the same time. When the drill starts they will move forward up the floor and make a through the legs move with one ball and a crossover with the other ball at the same time at the free throw line, half court, and opposite free throw line (alternate which hand makes the move each time). After each move they need to push the basketballs out in front of them, simulating getting past the defender. When they reach the opposite baseline they will turn around and come back.

Double Ball Dribbling Drills (advanced)

The 2 ball bounce between the legs crossover, behind the back crossover, and the double crossover basketball dribbling drills are really advanced drills, but they will help you work on strengthening both hands at the same time, developing quicker hands, and working on the between the legs, behind the back, and crossover move. This may not be the first two ball dribbling drill you do, but once you have mastered a few of the other two ball dribbling drills this is a great drill to test your skills on. It is important that you are always challenging yourself as a player and pushing yourself to get better on a regular basis. You can either practice this stationary or during a zig-zag drill.

Two Ball Attack Double Crossover Drill- The player is going to start on the baseline with a basketball in each hand. They are going to start dribbling and moving forward up the court. When they get to the first free throw line they are going to make a crossover move at the same time with both balls twice before moving forward again. They will repeat this move at the half court line and opposite free throw line as well (3 spots total). Once they get to the opposite baseline they will turn around and come back, repeating the same pattern.

Two Ball Attack Double Through the legs Drill- The player is going to start on the baseline with a basketball in each hand. They are going to start dribbling and moving forward up the court. When they get to the first free throw line they are going to make a crossover move with one ball and a through the legs with the other ball twice before moving forward again. They will repeat this move at the half court line and opposite free throw line as well. Once they get to the opposite baseline they will turn around and come back, repeating the same pattern.

Two Ball Attack Double Behind the Back Drill- The player is going to start on the baseline with a basketball in each hand. They are going to start dribbling and moving forward up the court. When they get to the first free throw line they are going to make a crossover move with one ball and a behind the back with the other ball twice before moving forward again. They will repeat this move at the half court line and opposite free throw line as well. Once they get to the opposite baseline they will turn around and come back, repeating the same pattern.
